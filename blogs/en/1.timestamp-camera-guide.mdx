## Core Concepts and Definition of Timestamp Cameras

**Timestamp cameras** are image processing tools that can automatically add time and date information to photos. Unlike traditional cameras, timestamp cameras not only record the shooting moment but also permanently embed time information into images in the form of watermarks. This technology is widely used in legal forensics, project management, social media sharing, and many other fields.

The core functions of timestamp cameras include automatic time synchronization, multiple time format display, customizable position and style, etc. They can ensure that the time information of photos cannot be tampered with, providing authoritative time proof for images. Whether you're a professional photographer or an ordinary user, you can benefit from the convenience and security they provide.

## Working Principles and Technical Implementation of Timestamp Cameras

The working principle of timestamp cameras is relatively simple but the technical implementation is quite precise. The system first obtains current time information, including precise data such as year, month, day, hour, minute, and second. Then, through image processing algorithms, it converts this time information into visual text or digital formats.

At the technical implementation level, timestamp cameras typically follow these steps: time data acquisition → format conversion → font rendering → position calculation → image synthesis. The entire process is completed at the millisecond level, ensuring users can see the timestamp effect in real-time. Modern timestamp cameras also support advanced features such as timezone adjustment and multi-language display.
